Thomas Cole: The Voyage of Life

One of my favorite series of paintings at the National Gallery was the Voyage of Life series by Thomas Cole. It showcased 4 paintings going through different stages of a person's life. The names of the paintings were Childhood, Youth, Adulthood, and Old Age. My favorite painting of the series was Old Age. I liked this painting the most because it was nostalgic and happy at the same time. It showed an old man reaching towards an angel from his boat. I thought that showed that the person was happy with their life/voyage, but ready to move on at the same time. The whole series was also interesting to me because it showed how a person's perspective will change with age.

Albert Bierstadt: The Last of the Buffalo

This was another one of my favorite paintings. I think that it is very detailed, and also a little bit creepy. If you look closely, you can see dead buffalo, horses, and people. I still liked the painting because of the prairie landscape, and all of the action in the painting.

Pierre-Auguste Renoir: Young Spanish Woman with a Guitar

This was also another one of my favorite paintings. It is very simple, but it's also abstract and dark. I like the hat that the woman is wearing, and I also like how the guitar was painted. Another thing I like is how happy the woman seems in the painting.

Vincent Van Gogh: Roses

I liked this painting for multiple reasons. I think it was very simple, but also detailed and abstract at the same time. I liked how the flowers were all one color, and they also complemented the vase that they were in. Another thing I liked were the swirls in the painting itself.

Eugene Delacroix: Tiger Growling at a Snake

I liked this painting , but it was not one of my all-time favorites. The tiger was my favorite part of this painting because it was so colorful. I also like how the snake curled around the tree. The most interesting part of this piece for me was the fact that a tiger and snake were actually fighting each other.
